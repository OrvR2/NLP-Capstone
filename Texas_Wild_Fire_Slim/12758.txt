On Alert For 9/11 Anniversary September 10, 2011 |  Filed underHeadlines |  Posted by Preston Richardson Terror Threat on 9/11 AnniversaryAs the anniversary of 9/11 is closing in, Al Qaeda terror threat is frightening New Yorkers and Washingtonians. Counterterrorism officials are working around the clock to determine whether the threat is accurate. Extra security was placed to protect the people in the two cities â€“ New York and Washington D.C. that took the brunt of the jetliner attacks which killed nearly 3,000 people at the World Trade Center and the Pentagon a decade ago. Al-Qaeda is rumored of striking again to mark the anniversary of the September 11 attack. The police are armed with assault rifles and commuters waited with varying degrees of patience at security checkpoints. Beginning Friday, intelligence officials scrambled to nail down information on a possible Al-Qaeda strike timed to coincide with the 10th anniversary of 9/11.New York Police Tightened SecurityNew York City police tightened their security around Manhattan beginning Friday. Investigators acted to corroborate the threat of a possible Al Qaeda terror attack in time for the 10th anniversary of Sept. 11 on Sunday. The investigative work expand their security operation in the metropolitan area that spread to Long Island and other areas on Friday. Investigators were stopping traffic and increasing patrols. Many investigators visit truck and vehicle rental outfits, as well as suppliers of fertilizer and other potential explosives for signs of any unusual activity that might relate to the latest threat about a car or truck bomb attack, said a law enforcement official.3 Unidentified Men Connected To Terror ThreatAccording to intelligence information, three unidentified men from Pakistan were connected on a terror attack in New York or Washington. Sources said Ayman al-Zawahiri who succeeded Osama bin Laden as leader of Al-Qaeda had been involved in the planning. The Associated Press reported that the three, at least one of whom was a U.S. citizen, may be traveling to the United States or have recently entered the country. The information was relayed to NYPD Commissioner Ray Kelly by the Joint Terrorism Task Force on Thursday afternoon, at which time he notified Mayor Michael Bloomberg, said police spokesman Paul Browne. In Washington, law enforcement officials said they were working 12-hour days indefinitely.
