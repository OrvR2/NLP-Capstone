FTSE rises 3.14% after German court rulingAFP – 18 hrs ago




Related ContentEuropean stock markets closed higher after Germany's top court approved eurozone …

European stock markets rallied on Wednesday after Germany's top court approved eurozone bailouts, with London's FTSE 100 index of leading companies climbing 3.14 percent. 
The German court ruling paved the way for Germany, the European Union's de facto paymaster, to continue its multi-billion-euro contributions to bailouts.
London's FTSE 100 index of leading companies climbed 3.14 percent to 5,318.59 points
Elsewhere in Europe, France's CAC jumped 3.63 percent to 3,073.18 points and in Frankfurt the DAX rose 4.07 percent to 5,405.53 points.
In London, Lloyds was the most widely traded stock with 156 million shares changing hands, followed by Royal Bank of Scotland (RBS) with 91.4 million shares. 
Tullow Oil was the day's best performer, gaining 7.83 percent (85 pence) to finish at 117.1. 
It was followed by Petrofac which rose 7. 25 percent (93 pence) to close at 137.4.
Miner Randgold Resources the worst performer of the day, shedding 2.3 percent (160 pence) to finish at 678. It was followed by Imperial Tobacco which was down 1.9 percent (40 pence) to close at  206.5. 
On the currency markets, a pound was worth 1.593 dollars or 1.134 euros at 16.13 GMT.
