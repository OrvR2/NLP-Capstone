Teen Mother's Plea Bargain Hits Snag Embed this VideoxEmailFacebookDiggTwitterRedditDelicious Link 
SAN ANTONIO -- Just as Julie Navejar was about to plead no contest to charges of injury to a child by omission, the hearing came to an abrupt halt.Judge Carmen Kelsey questioned whether the charge was a first- or a second-degree felony.Navejar's attorney said his understanding was that it was a second-degree felony.Navejar's plea bargain called for a plea to a second-degree felony and a maximum sentence of 12 years.The teen and her boyfriend, Ramiro De la Rosa, 18, the infant's father, were charged with injury to a child by omission after their 7-week-old daughter, Jada, was found by paramedics outside a West side fire station. She was dead.De la Rosa remains jailed awaiting trial.Navejar's hearing will continue on Monday.
