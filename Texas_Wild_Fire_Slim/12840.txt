DANGEROUS WILDFIRE CONDITIONS PREDICTED NEAR THE TEXAS PANHANDLE ON THURSDAY









[ Print | 

 ]



March 16, 2011 – College Station, TEXAS – Texas Forest Service officials are predicting that weather conditions on Thursday, March 17, could lead to dangerous wildfire in the Texas Panhandle and north and west of a dry line from Wichita Falls down to Midland and Van Horn, including the Guadalupe Mountains. In this area of West and Northwest Texas, temperatures are predicted to be in the 90-degree range. Relative humidity is expected to be 10 percent or less, and winds are predicted to be 15 to 20 mph with gusts up to 30 mph.The rest of the state will see windy conditions with lower temperatures and higher humidity.In the past seven days, Texas Forest Service responded to 67 fires burning 14,726 acres. So far this year, the agency has responded to 364 fires burning 246,089 acres. More than 1,750 structures have been saved, and 170 structures have been lost. Of the 254 counties in Texas, 169 currently are reporting burn bans. Residents should avoid outdoor activities that could generate a spark or open flames. Even if your area has had recent rain relief, it could still be in danger of wildfire. Clear combustible materials away from your home and don’t do any outdoor burning until the threat of fire decreases and burn bans are lifted.Monitor the current wildfire status.
