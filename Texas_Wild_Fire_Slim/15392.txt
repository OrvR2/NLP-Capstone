Frustration grows for Texas wildfire evacuees
 September 10th, 2011  WN.com - World News 

								
				CHRISTOPHER SHERMAN The Associated Press BASTROP, Texas - Residents left homeless by a massive Central Texas wildfire turned their attention Friday to what they need to move forward, with some voicing frustration over a perceived delay in federal response even as early signs of recovery appeared in reopened neighborhoods. Firefighters focused on extinguishing hotspots and had isolated remaining flames from the blaze that has burned for almost a week in and around the city of Bastrop, destroying nearly 1,400 homes and sweeping across about 45 square miles of rain-starved landscape. "We believe the forward progress (of the fire) has been stopped, thank God for that," Lt. Gov. David Dewhurst...
