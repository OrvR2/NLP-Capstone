However, they are still struggling against a larger fire coming down the mountains toward another exclusive suburb. With temperatures above 100 Fahrenheit, flames flared above La Canada Flintridge, 12 miles from downtown Los Angeles and home to NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory. Nearly 900 homes have been evacuated and 1,500 acres have burned in just two days. "It is very active but hasn't reached the neighborhoods yet," said Gabriel Alvarez of the U.S. Forest Service. Aircraft have been dropping water and retardant on the steep terrain, while some residents stayed behind to hose down the roofs, trees and lawns of their multimillion-dollar homes. California Governor Arnold Schwarzenegger declared a state of emergency for Los Angeles and Monterey counties due to wildfires that have burned 13,000 acres. Two more large fires burned further inland, making this week the worst conflagration in Los Angeles since November 2008, when multiple fires burned hundreds of dwellings.» More on this story... «ITN
