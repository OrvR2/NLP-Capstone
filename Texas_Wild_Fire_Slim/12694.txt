Japan Minister Hachiro was only eight days into his tenure. Hachiro already tendered his letter of resignation to Prime Minister Yoshihiko Noda. According to reports, Hachiro’s resignation is a blow to Noda, who took office and launched his Cabinet on Sept. 2. Hachiro scheduled to hold a press conference later Saturday.Hachiro Came Under Pressure Over GaffeAfter a week after that the Industry Minister of Japan took oath of office, Hachiro came under pressure to quit after a joke about radiation in Fukushima. Hachiro was greatly terrified after calling areas near the crippled Fukushima No. 1 nuclear plant a “ghost town” on Friday and joking with reporters about radiation leaking from the plant causing a public uproar. Hachiro had apologized for the remarks, pledging to “work hard” as economy, trade and industry minister. But despite the apology, the policy chief of the Liberal Democratic Party Shigeru Ishiba assailed Hachiro over his comments. Ishiba told Hachiro that if he didn’t offer to resign, PM Noda will fire him.Hachiro’s Apology To The Public FailedHachiro apologized to the public but ultimately failed. That was the time when the industry minister, Yoshio Hachiro, stepped down after apologies failed to quell calls for his resignation within his own governing Democratic Party. The party appeared to be moving quickly to control damage to Mr. Noda’s government. This was the first political setback of Prime Minister Yoshihiko Noda of Japan when the new minister of trade and industry resigned after a joke about radiation caused a public uproar. Hachiro’s comments were not limited to residents in the crisis-affected areas and to opposition party lawmakers. He was also rebuked by some of his colleagues in the ruling Democratic Party of Japan.The Nuclear Plant GaffeThe problem began on Thursday, after Mr. Hachiro returned from a trip to evacuated areas around the stricken Fukushima Daiichi nuclear plant. According to Japanese newspaper reports, Mr. Hachiro, who was wearing protective clothing, moved as if to wipe his sleeve against a reporter and jokingly said, “Look out, radiation!” The jest caused outrage largely because Mr. Hachiro’s ministry. Hachiro called the evacuated communities around the plants “dead towns,” a statement seen as insensitive to the approximately 80,000 people who had been driven from their homes by the nuclear accident. Mr. Hachiro apologized the same day for that remark. But then he made the comment about radiation on his clothing, which was criticized in newspapers on Saturday as a serious lapse of judgment. Mr. Hachiro later said that his recollection of the episode was “not very clear,” though he admitted that he had pretended to rub his sleeve on a reporter.In this article you learned that Japan Industry Minister Yoshio Hachiro resigned from his post Saturday after making controversial remarks about the crisis at the Fukushima No. 1 nuclear plant. Hachiro was in charge of promoting as well as regulating nuclear power that has been widely blamed for lax oversight that allowed the Daiichi plant to operate without adequate defenses against tsunamis.
