Strasburg comes back strong, allows 2 hits in 5 innings in 1st start since Tommy John surgery

Posted on September 7, 2011 by Associated Press 

This article has been syndicated.
WASHINGTON — Nationals pitcher Stephen Strasburg allowed two hits over five scoreless innings Tuesday night in his first major league start since undergoing Tommy John surgery.
His workload carefully monitored, Strasburg threw 56 pitches, 40 for strikes. He struck out four and his fastball peaked at 99 mph.
Strasburg left with the Nationals leading 3-0, but he was handed a no-decision when the Dodgers tied it in the top of the sixth.
