AGOIncident OverviewImage options: [ Enlarge ] [ Full Size ]Consistent Fire Numbers are provided each Monday and Friday; as of September 12, 2011:For the period of 9/5/11- 9/11/11 Local fire departments fought 241 fires for 6,450 acres. Homes saved 168, homes lost 2; other structures 18 - total lost 20.For the period of 9/5/11 - 9/11/11 Texas Forest Service fought 141 fires for 34,943 acres. Homes saved 2,095, homes lost 136; other structures 59 - total lost 195.Since January 1, 2011 to 9/11/11 Local Fire Department responded to 17,037 fire for total acerage of 885,824. Homes saved 10,701, other structures 9,602. Homes lost 219, other structures 921. From January 1, 2011 to 9/11/11 Texas Forest Service responded to 2,859 fire for 2,809,161 acres. Homes saved 21,939, other structures 7,500. Homes lost 2411, other structure 1,128.Yesterday September 13, Texas Forest Service responded to 35 new fires for 9,752 acres including new large fires in Harris and Trinity counties. In the past seven days, Texas Forest Service has responded to 149 fires for 17,334 acres.250 of the 254 Texas counties are reporting burn bans. Four Military C-130 aircraft are in place at Austin-Bergstrom Airport. These large airtankers can deliver more than 3,000 gallons per load.Daily detailed fire information can be found at http://texasforestservice.tamu.edu/main/article.aspx id=12888 or at www.inciweb.org.
