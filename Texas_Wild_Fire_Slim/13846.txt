 Mobile data usage in Canada to triple between 2010 and 2012 and Soars by 2015

Some interesting, yet not terribly surprising, numbers on mobile usage coming out of Canada. According to a report summarized on Techvibes, mobile usage is soaring, in particular, the smartphone segment. Some highlights from the feature:

more than 24.5 million mobile subscribers – not bad for a country of less than 40 million
One third of subscribers own smartphones (think data consumption)
47% between the age of 18-44
Data usage will set a record in 2012 with over 18.4 Pedabytes being consumed (that’s 3X the amount from 2010) and that soars to 71.5 by 2015
Consumers are expecting to connect with “brands on the go”
Websites built with Flash are a constant frustration – think opportunity for mobile developers!
Over 3/4 of mobile searches have local intent
Only 18% of websites are optimized for mobile



Percentage of users who access the Internet via phone – Image Credit: TechVibes
See more details on mobile usage in Canada on Techvibes – Research provided via http://www.brickandmobile.com/
