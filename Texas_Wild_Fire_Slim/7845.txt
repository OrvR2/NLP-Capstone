Egypt placed a police force and declared a state of alert for the overnight violence. Protesters broke inside the Israeli embassy tearing down a cement barrier around the building. Six Israeli embassy staff were trapped inside the the building.Israel’s Ambassador With Family And Staff EvacuatedIsrael’s Ambassador to Egypt and senior staff were evacuated back to their home country after the angry protesters attacked the building on Friday night. Together with Israel Ambassador’s family and most of their staff and dependents, which were around 80 people were taken out of Cairo by a military aircraft. The plane which carry Yitzhak Levanon, the ambassador of Israel to Egypt and others had landed safely in Israel as reported by Israel Radio.Israel’s Consul Left In CairoIsrael’s consul for state affairs was left behind to maintain its embassy in Cairo, Egypt. Israel weighed its response to the attack by dozens of protesters on the building that houses its diplomatic mission. The AFP news agency reported that Egyptian commandos had entered the embassy building as it was attacked by protesters to escort six Israeli citizens to safety.Obama Calls Egypt To Observe International ObligationsUS President Barack Obama earlier called on Egypt to “honour its international obligations” in order to protect the diplomats. President Obama told Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu that Washington was taking steps to resolve the situation. US secretary of state Hillary Clinton also spoke with Egyptian foreign minister Mohamed Kamel Amr, who cut short a trip to Poland as Essam Sharaf, the Egyptian prime minister to conduct a crisis cabinet meeting to respond to the attack. An Israeli government official who wanted to remain anonymous told the Al Jazeera bureau in Jerusalem that Israel was grateful to Obama and Egyptian government for helping to resolve the situation.Egypt Committed Grave Violation Of Diplomatic NormsThe incident was a blow to Israeli-Egyptian relations and a grave violation of accepted diplomatic behavior. More so, the attack to the Israeli Embassy in Cairo was a blatantly against international diplomatic norms. The rampage further worsened the already deteriorating ties between Israel and post-Hosni Mubarak Egypt.
