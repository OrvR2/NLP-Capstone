Bastrop Co. kids head back to schoolSchools serve free food for all students all weekUpdated: Monday, 12 Sep 2011, 7:08 AM CDTPublished : Monday, 12 Sep 2011, 5:56 AM CDT
Erin Cargile 

BASTROP, Texas (KXAN) - One week after fire wiped out more than 1500 homes in Bastrop County, children will head back to school today. "We're going to have a large increase in our students who are officially designated as homeless, we understand that and there's specific support that we can give those students," said Bastrop ISD Superintendent Steve Murray at a press conference Sunday.  Bastrop  and  Smithville  school districts will resume a regular schedule and offer all students free breakfast and lunch all week. Both districts have added extra bus routes to make it easier on families staying in hotels and at emergency shelters.  Bastrop ISD:  Best Western Elementary Pick up - 6:40 AM (Bus 304) BIS & BMS Pick up - 7:45 AM (Bus 123) BHS Pick up - 7:25 AM (Bus 340)  Holiday Inn Elementary Pick up - 6:35 AM (Bus 340) BIS & BMS Pick up - 7:15 AM (Bus 101) BHS Pick up - 8:10 AM (Bus 340)  Super 8 Elementary Pick up - 6:35 AM (Bus 340) BIS & BMS Pick up - 7:15 AM (Bus 101) BHS Pick up - 8:10 AM (Bus 340)  Hyatt Lost Pines Elementary Pick up - 8:15 AM (Bus 326) BIS & BMS Pick up - 8:15 AM (Bus 326) BHS Pick up - 8:15 AM (Bus 326)  Smithville ISD: Bus routes will run at regular times. Buses will pick up displaced students at Smithville Recreation Center and First Baptist Church between 7:30 and 8:00 A.M. Displaced students temporarily housed outside the Smithville ISD boundaries, please contact the SISD offices at 512-237-2487 for pick up arrangements. The Smithville ISD Employee Relief Fund for staff and students has been established at First National Bank of Bastrop. Cash donations can be made at any First National Bank branch. Cash or gift card donations can be made at any Smithville ISD office.  Click here for the Smithville ISD website   Click here for the Bastrop ISD website
