CAPE CANAVERAL, Fla. -- NASA announced Tuesday it will eventually need 300 people to launch a new rocket from Kennedy Space Center.The Liberty Rocket is expected to carry astronauts and cargo to the International Space Station by 2015.Tuesday afternoon, NASA said it is partnering with Utah-based company ATK to develop the new rocket.ATK used to build the solid rocket boosters for the shuttles.Liberty will use many of the existing buildings and platforms are KSC and draw from the experience of many shuttle workers to launch the rocket.Thousands of space coast jobs were lost when the shuttle program retired earlier this year.
