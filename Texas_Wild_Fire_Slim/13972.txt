Austin is responding to the Central Texas wildfires with benefits and donations. Here's a partial list; look for more ways to give on our Facebook page, Central Texas Wildfire Recovery.At the Nutty Brown Cafe on Saturday, Will Taylor and Strings Attached will collect medical supplies, bedding, toiletries, nonperishable food and gently used clothes. The show starts at 7:30 p.m. The lineup includes Jon Dee Graham, Karen Mal and Robert Kraft. strings attached.org.• On Sunday, the Texas Wildfire Relief Fund will have a vigil to raise money for first responders battling the wildfires and to commemorate the 10th anniversary of 9/11. 8 to 9 p.m. on the south steps of the Capitol.On Sunday, Shelley King, Wendy Colonna, John Pointer, Mark Allen Atwood, the Chevelles, Ginger Leigh, Will Dunlap and others will have a benefit from noon to 8 p.m. at the Red Shed, 8504 S. Congress Ave. Donations of nonperishable food items, clothing, toys, diapers and other toiletries accepted for the Red Cross of Central Texas. Cost: $15.Red 7, 611 E. Seventh St., is having an ongoing benefit drive with the Red Cross and Austin Christian Fellowship. Bring donations to the venue anytime during business hours (4 p.m.-2 a.m. through Sunday). Items accepted include bottled water, sports drinks, canned foods, toiletries, hand sanitizer, work gloves, trash bags, flashlights, batteries, pet food, clothing, blankets, pillows, sleeping bags, paper plates, cups, napkins, plastic utensils and baby shampoo.Carter Beckworth's show Friday at Threadgill's South is now a benefit. Any donation of money or supplies will get someone into the show for free. Organizers are also trying to gather autographed items from Austin bands for an auction.Fun Fun Fun Fest will collect donations from noon to 6 p.m. Sunday at the Fun Fun Fun Water Olympics at Fiesta Gardens. Items can include clothing, canned food, bottled water, Tylenol/Advil/pain relievers, baby food, trash cans/trash bags, laundry detergent, socks, dog food, cat food, crates, cat litter, toys, towels and dog beds. Organizers ask that items be placed in separate bags or boxes for easy sorting. The event is free. funfunfunfest.com.Rae Cosmetics (1206 W. 38th St. 320-8732) is working with Catholic Charities of Central Texas to collect donations. Accepted items: water, nonperishable food, clothing, trash bags, flashlights, batteries, blankets, pillows, towels, shampoo, body wash, toothpaste, paper plates, cups, napkins, plastic utensils, deodorant, disposable razors, shaving cream, baby wipes, size 4 diapers, baby shampoo, sanitizer, Depends undergarments and sleeping bags. Hours are 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. through Saturday.Austin makeup artist Kandy Glass of FaceKandy is collecting donations to be delivered to Covert Auto Group in Bastrop at the end of the week. Donations can include children's clothes, backpacks, toiletries and adult clothing, especially workplace attire. Drop off at Studio Downtown (608 E. Third St.) or Cypress Vanguard Fashion House (606 E. Third St.) next to Moonshine Patio Bar & Grill during business hours.There are collection centers in the Second Street District. Locations include Malaga, La Condesa, Lofty Dog and Plain Ivey Jane, which will take donations during business hours through Sunday during the district's Second Sunday events.Russ & Company Salon (1805 West Ave. 472-6961, www.russandcompanysalon.com) is accepting donations for fire crews and fire victims such as water, Gatorade, nonperishable snacks such as protein bars and nuts, heavy-duty trash bags, work gloves, toiletries, towels, sunscreen, clothes, shoes, bedding, flashlights with batteries, and pet food.Heirloom Tea Room at Bastrop Gardens Organic Garden Center, 316 Old 71 in Cedar Creek, is turning a grand-opening party from 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. Saturday into a fundraiser. Owner Deena Spellman also making the center a sanctuary with classical music and teas. (512) 303-5672, www.bastropgardens.com.• The Dirty Dog Bar, 505 E. Sixth, will host a benefit with live music and raffles from 2 to 10 p.m. Sunday. $5 donation at the door. All proceeds benefit wildfire relief efforts.Austin Variety Show raised $3,500 in cash and donations this week and plans to raise more at its Sept. 17 show. Twenty percent of ticket sales will go to relief efforts, and additional donations will be accepted. www.austin varietyshow.com.BookPeople is accepting money donations and is donating a portion of sales through Friday to the Red Cross.• From 9 a.m. to 9 p.m. Sunday, Bella Salon & Spa (1221 W. Sixth St. 474-5999, www.bellasalonaustin.com) will have a cut-a-thon and clothing drive for area wildfire victims. There's a minimum $30 for shampoo and cut and $50 for shampoo cut and styling. Ten percent of proceeds will be donated.• Centro-matic will donate proceeds from sales of Centro-matic/South San Gabriel/Will Johnson merchandise purchased on the Undertow Music Collective website on Saturday and Sunday. Frontman Will Johnson will also auction two of his baseball paintings starting Sunday. Johnson recently relocated to Austin from Bastrop and said the area influenced his songwriting.Addie Broyles, Sharon Chapman, Marques Harper and Peter Mongillo contributed to this report.
