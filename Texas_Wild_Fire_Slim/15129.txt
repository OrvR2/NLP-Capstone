He met with emergency management and local authorities who have been combating the fires in the area. Rick Perry called on all Texans to stay up to date with conditions and pay attention to warnings from local authorities. Strong winds and the generally dry weather are expected bolster the wildfires raging across Texas.Governor Releases Statement On Texas FireBelow are snippets from the Texas governor’s statement. “These fires are serious and widespread, and as mean as I have ever seen, burning more than 1,000 homes since this wildfire season began,” the governor said. “Texas appreciates the resources and support we continue to receive from across the state and across the country to fight these fires, and the efforts of the brave men and women who put themselves in harm’s way to protect Texans’ lives and property. Our thoughts and prayers are with those who are impacted by these fires,” he added. “Our primary focus is on safety and protecting lives, and Texans need to heed all warnings, especially evacuation warnings from local officials,” said Nim Kidd, Texas Emergency Management Chief. “The Texas Division of Emergency Management is actively supporting local firefighting efforts across the state, and we will continue to provide all necessary assistance.”TIFMAS ActivatedMoreover, the Texas Intrastate Fire Mutual Aid System (TIFMAS), has been activated. TIFMAS is a network of state fire departments ready to provide emergency resources to surrounding communities. TIFMAS has deployed 18 command vehicles, 72 fire trucks and 121 personnel in Central Texas.Texas Fires Burned Down Thousands Of AcresTexas Forest Service (TFS) has combated about 181 fires that have burned more than 118,400 acres in the past week. These include fires in Bastrop, Henderson, Limestone, Caldwell, and Grimes counties. TFS is also combating existing major wildfires in Palo Pinto, Briscoe, and Montague counties. Since the start of wildfire season, state and local firefighters have battled a recorded 20,900 fires. These fires have destroyed about 1,000 homes and burned an estimated 3.6 million acres. Governor Rick Perry has for the ninth time issued a disaster proclamation. The declaration was first issued on December 21, 2010.In this article, you learned that governor Rick Perry went on an aerial tour to survey the wildfire damage in central Texas. Snippets from the statement released by the governor are offered.
